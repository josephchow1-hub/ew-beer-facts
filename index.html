Light & CrispLager · Wheat · Kölsch
Who it's forCustomers who want something refreshing and easy-drinking. Wine drinkers trying beer for the first time. Hot day, patio, anything goes.
Tap to learn
Lager Pilsner Kölsch Wheat Beer Session Ale
Lager
FlavourClean, crisp, neutral. Mild grain sweetness, low bitterness, very refreshing. The world's most popular beer style.
ABV4–5%
GlasswarePint glass or stein. Always serve cold (2–4°C).
Examples
Stella ArtoisHeinekenCoronaPeroni
BC Craft
Parallel 49 LagerSteamworks LagerFour Winds Nectarous
Pairs with
PizzaBurgersFried chickenSpicy food
💬 How to sell it: "If you want something cold, easy, and refreshing — a lager is the classic choice. Light, clean, no surprises. Great with any food."
Pilsner
FlavourCrisp, dry, slightly hoppy. More character than standard lager — floral and herbal hop aroma, clean bitterness, golden colour.
ABV4–5.5%
StylesCzech Pilsner (Bohemian) — fuller, rounder, soft water. German Pilsner — drier, crisper, more bitter.
Examples
Pilsner UrquellBitburgerWarsteinerKronenbourg 1664
BC Craft
Dageraad BurnabarianBrassneck Passive AggressiveStrange Fellows Talisman
Pairs with
SeafoodSausagesLight saladsSchnitzel
💬 How to sell it: "A pilsner is like a lager with more personality — still light and crisp but with a little more hop flavour and aroma. The original craft beer, honestly."
Kölsch
FlavourLight, clean, subtly fruity. Ale fermented cold — hybrid character. Pale gold, soft fruit esters, gentle bitterness. Very approachable.
ABV4.4–5.2%
OriginCologne (Köln), Germany. Traditionally served in tall, narrow 200ml glasses called Stangen.
Examples
Reissdorf KölschFrüh KölschGaffel Kölsch
BC Craft
Andina KolschHowe Sound KölschSuperflux Kölsch
Pairs with
Grilled fishLight pastaSaladsMild cheese
💬 How to sell it: "Kölsch is one of the most drinkable beers there is — light like a lager but with a tiny bit of fruitiness from being brewed like an ale. If someone likes Sauvignon Blanc, they'll love a Kölsch."
Wheat Beer
FlavourHazy, soft, fruity. Banana and clove from yeast (Hefeweizen), or citrusy and light (American Wheat). Creamy mouthfeel, low bitterness.
ABV4–5.5%
StylesHefeweizen (German) — banana, clove, unfiltered, hazy. Witbier (Belgian) — coriander, orange peel, hazy. American Wheat — lighter, less yeast character.
Examples
Paulaner HefeweizenErdingerBlue MoonHoegaarden
BC Craft
Granville Island Robson HefeweizenR&B East Side WheatSteamworks Pumphouse Wheat
Pairs with
SeafoodSaladsBrunchLight desserts
💬 How to sell it: "Wheat beers are soft, hazy, and a little fruity — almost like a smoothie of the beer world. German ones taste like banana bread; Belgian ones like orange and spice. Great for people who find regular beer too bitter."
Session Ale
FlavourLight, easy-drinking, low alcohol. Can be hoppy, malty, or fruity depending on style. Designed to drink multiple pints without getting too full or too drunk.
ABV3.5–4.5% — "session" means drinkable all session
Examples
Founders All Day IPAFirestone Easy JackTennent's
BC Craft
Parallel 49 TricycleFour Winds Session IPAPostmark Session Ale
Pairs with
Anything casualPub foodBBQ
💬 How to sell it: "Session ales are made for drinking — lower alcohol, easy, refreshing. Great choice if someone wants to have a few beers without going overboard."
Quick tipIf a customer says "I just want something light" — lager or Kölsch. "Something refreshing but with a bit more flavour" — Pilsner or Wheat Beer.
Hop-ForwardIPA · Pale Ale · Hoppy
Who it's forCustomers who say "I like something hoppy" or "give me something with flavour." The most popular craft beer category in BC.
Tap to learn
Pale Ale IPA West Coast IPA Hazy / NEIPA Double IPA Session IPA
Pale Ale
FlavourBalanced hop and malt. Citrus, floral, light pine. Gateway to hoppy beer — not too bitter, not too heavy. The most accessible IPA alternative.
ABV4.5–5.5%
Examples
Sierra Nevada Pale AleCoopers Pale AleFuller's London Pride
BC Craft
Granville Island Pale AleCentral City Red Racer PaleTofino Blonde Ale
Pairs with
BurgersGrilled chickenMild curryCheddar
💬 How to sell it: "A pale ale is the perfect middle ground — more flavour than a lager but way less bitter than an IPA. If someone is new to craft beer, this is where I'd start them."
IPA — India Pale Ale
FlavourAssertively hoppy, bitter, aromatic. Citrus, pine, resin, tropical fruit. The backbone of craft beer.
ABV5.5–7.5%
HistoryOriginally brewed strong and heavily hopped for the voyage from England to India — hops act as a preservative.
Examples
Stone IPALagunitas IPAGoose Island IPA
BC Craft
Phillips Analogue IPABomber Brewing IPADriftwood Fat Tug IPA
Pairs with
Spicy foodStrong cheeseGrilled meatsBBQ
💬 How to sell it: "IPA is the craft beer world's most popular style — bold, hoppy, aromatic. The bitterness is actually refreshing, like a dry wine. If they love a crisp Sauvignon Blanc or gin, the hop aroma will appeal to them."
West Coast IPA
FlavourDry, bitter, piney, resinous. Clear and golden. More bitter and drier than East Coast/Hazy styles. This is the BC craft beer signature style.
ABV6–7.5%
HopsCascade, Centennial, Chinook, Simcoe, Amarillo. Pine, grapefruit, resin.
Examples
Pliny the ElderKnee Deep Breaking BudBallast Point Sculpin
BC Craft
Four Winds IPABrassneck Passive AggressiveYellow Dog Fetch IPA
Pairs with
Fish tacosGrilled salmonStrong aged cheese
💬 How to sell it: "West Coast IPA is what BC craft beer is known for — dry, bitter, piney, refreshing. If someone is a craft beer fan visiting from out of town, this is what they should try first."
Hazy IPA / NEIPA
FlavourSoft, juicy, tropical. Low bitterness despite being hoppy. Tastes like drinking tropical fruit juice. Hazy/cloudy appearance from unfiltered dry hopping.
ABV5.5–8%
HopsCitra, Mosaic, Galaxy, El Dorado — New World hops with tropical, mango, passionfruit character.
Examples
Tree House JuliusAlchemist Heady TopperVoodoo Ranger Juicy Haze
BC Craft
Superflux Juicy IPAFour Winds NectarousStrathcona Hazy IPA
Pairs with
Spicy ThaiSushiGrilled shrimpMango dishes
💬 How to sell it: "Hazy IPAs are the most approachable hoppy beer — they're fruity and juicy, almost like tropical fruit juice, with barely any bitterness. If someone thinks they don't like IPAs, a hazy will change their mind."
Double IPA (DIPA / Imperial IPA)
FlavourIntensely hoppy, high alcohol, full body. Everything an IPA is but turned up. Can be bitter and resinous (West Coast DIPA) or juicy and tropical (Hazy DIPA).
ABV7.5–10%+ — sip slowly
Examples
Pliny the ElderDogfish Head 90 MinStone Ruination
BC Craft
Driftwood Sartori DIPACentral City Thor's HammerBomber DIPA
Pairs with
Strong aged cheeseSpicy wingsRich BBQ
💬 How to sell it: "A Double IPA is for the serious hop lover — twice the hops, higher alcohol, more of everything. Treat it like a wine — sip it, don't chug it. Usually sold in smaller formats for good reason."
Session IPA
FlavourHoppy aroma and flavour at low alcohol. Citrus, tropical, light pine. All the IPA character without the weight.
ABV3.5–4.5%
Examples
Founders All Day IPAFirestone Easy JackDogfish Head Slightly Mighty
BC Craft
Four Winds Session IPAParallel 49 Session IPAPostmark Session IPA
Pairs with
SaladsLight appetizersCasual food
💬 How to sell it: "All the hop flavour of an IPA at half the alcohol — great for someone who wants to enjoy a couple of beers over a long lunch or patio session without feeling it."
Quick tip"Not too bitter" → Pale Ale or Hazy IPA. "I love hoppy beer" → West Coast IPA. "Big and bold" → Double IPA.
Dark & RoastyStout · Porter · Dark Lager
Who it's forCoffee and chocolate lovers. Customers who say "I like something dark and rich." Red wine drinkers looking for a beer equivalent.
Tap to learn
Stout Imperial Stout Porter Schwarzbier Dark Lager
Stout
FlavourDark, roasty, coffee, chocolate. Smooth and creamy (especially Milk Stout). Dry finish (Dry Irish Stout). Surprisingly light-bodied for the dark colour.
ABV4–6% (standard) · Milk Stout slightly sweeter
StylesDry Irish Stout — Guinness style, roasty, bitter finish. Milk Stout — lactose added, sweet and creamy. Oatmeal Stout — silky, smooth.
Examples
Guinness DraughtMurphy's Irish StoutLeft Hand Milk Stout
BC Craft
Parallel 49 Ugly SweaterHowe Sound Total EclipseSteamworks Heroica Oatmeal
Pairs with
OystersChocolate cakeBBQ ribsStrong cheese
💬 How to sell it: "Stout looks intimidating but it's actually lighter than it looks — Guinness for example is lower in calories than a lager. It tastes like coffee and dark chocolate. Oysters and stout is a classic pairing."
Imperial / Russian Imperial Stout
FlavourMassively complex — dark chocolate, espresso, dark fruit, molasses, vanilla (if barrel-aged), warming alcohol. The most intense beer style. Often barrel-aged in whisky or bourbon barrels.
ABV8–15%+ — treat like a dessert wine or spirit
Examples
Founders KBSGoose Island Bourbon CountyDeschutes The Abyss
BC Craft
Central City Thor's HammerHowe Sound Pothole FillerYellow Dog Imperial Stout
Pairs with
Dark chocolateStiltonCrème brûléeOn its own
💬 How to sell it: "Imperial Stout is the Port wine of the beer world — rich, complex, high alcohol, best in small glasses. Often barrel-aged in whisky casks, so whisky fans absolutely love it. A dessert in a glass."
Porter
FlavourRoasty but slightly lighter than stout. Chocolate, toffee, caramel, light coffee. Smooth and balanced. The predecessor to stout.
ABV4.5–6.5%
Examples
Fuller's London PorterAnchor PorterDeschutes Black Butte
BC Craft
Granville Island Lions Winter AleHowe Sound Diamond Head Oatmeal PorterPhillips Hammer Imperial Porter
Pairs with
Smoked meatsMushroom dishesChocolateAged cheddar
💬 How to sell it: "Think of a porter as a stout's slightly more approachable sibling — same chocolate and coffee notes but a little lighter. Great for someone who wants to try a dark beer without going full stout."
Schwarzbier (Black Lager)
FlavourDark colour, light body. Clean lager character with hints of roasted malt. Very approachable — much lighter than it looks. The "gateway dark beer."
ABV3.8–5%
Examples
Köstritzer SchwarzbierModelo NegraSapporo Black
BC Craft
Dageraad Burnabarian DarkAndina Schwarzbier
Pairs with
Grilled sausagesDark breadRoast pork
💬 How to sell it: "Schwarzbier is a dark lager — it looks like a stout but drinks like a lager. Super clean, crisp, just a hint of roast. Perfect for someone curious about dark beer but worried about it being too heavy."
Dark Lager / Dunkel
FlavourMalty, smooth, toasted bread, caramel, light chocolate. Clean lager finish. Munich-style, very drinkable. Less roasty than Schwarzbier.
ABV4.5–5.5%
Examples
Ayinger DunkelSpaten DunkelNegra Modelo
BC Craft
Steamworks DunkelPowell Brewery Dunkel
Pairs with
Roast chickenPretzelsSausagesSmoked meats
💬 How to sell it: "A Dunkel is a dark lager that tastes like liquid pumpernickel bread — toasty, malty, smooth. Not bitter at all, just warm and comforting. Great for autumn and winter."
Quick tip"Worried it'll be too heavy" → Schwarzbier or Dunkel. "Love coffee and chocolate" → Stout or Porter. "Want something special" → Imperial Stout.
Malt & SweetAmber · Brown · Märzen
Who it's forCustomers who want something fuller and more flavourful than a lager but not hoppy or dark. Great gateway for wine drinkers — toffee, caramel, biscuit flavours.

Amber Ale

Red Ale / Irish Red
FlavourMalty, slightly sweet, minimal hops. Caramel, toffee, biscuit, light roast. Red/copper colour. Smooth and easy. Irish Red = more malt-forward and sessionable.
ABV4–6%

Brown Ale
FlavourNutty, chocolatey, toffee, light roast. More malt-forward than amber, less roasty than stout. Dry (English) or sweet and nutty (American).
ABV4–6%
Examples

Märzen / Oktoberfest
FlavourRich, toasty, malty, clean lager finish. Amber colour, bready malt, low hop bitterness. Brewed in March (März), lagered through summer, released in September.
ABV5–6%
Examples

Bock
FlavourStrong lager, rich malt, minimal hops. Dark fruit, toffee, chocolate (Doppelbock), warming. Originally brewed as a strong lager for sustenance.
ABVBock 6–7% · Doppelbock 7–12%
StylesBock — amber, malty. Doppelbock — double strength, very rich (Paulaner Salvator). Maibock — pale, spring seasonal.
Examples

💬 How to sell it: "Bock is a strong, rich German lager — think of it as the cognac of lagers. Warming, complex, malty. Monks brewed it to sustain themselves through Lent. Great for a cold evening."
Quick tip"Something in the middle" → Amber Ale. "Autumn/winter vibes" → Märzen or Bock. "Nutty and smooth" → Brown Ale or Red Ale.
Sour & WildLambic · Gose · Berliner
Who it's forWine drinkers, adventurous customers, anyone who says "something different." Natural wine lovers. Sours are the fastest-growing beer category in BC craft.
Tap to learn
Berliner Weisse Gose Lambic Gueuze Fruit Sour Flanders Red
Berliner Weisse
FlavourTart, light, refreshing. Lactic sourness, wheat, lemony. Low alcohol. Like a sour lemonade. Often served with fruit syrups in Germany (raspberry or woodruff).
ABV2.8–3.8% — very sessionable
Examples
Professor Fritz Briem 1809Dogfish Head Festina Peche
BC Craft
Four Winds BerlinerParallel 49 Sour Cherry BerlinerSuperflux Berliner
Pairs with
SaladsSeafoodLight dessertsBrunch
💬 How to sell it: "Berliner Weisse is like sparkling lemonade with a tiny bit of alcohol — refreshing, tart, very light. Perfect for people who want something unusual but approachable. Great patio beer."
Gose
FlavourTart, slightly salty, wheat. Lemon, coriander, sea salt finish. The salt enhances flavour without tasting "salty." Very refreshing.
ABV4–5%
Examples
Anderson Valley The KimmieWestbrook GoseAvery El Gose
BC Craft
Strange Fellows GoseBrassneck GoseDogwood Gose
Pairs with
OystersCevicheFish tacosSalty snacks
💬 How to sell it: "Gose has a tiny bit of salt in it — like a margarita rim on a beer. It sounds weird but it makes it incredibly refreshing. Wine drinkers who like crisp, mineral whites (Chablis, Albariño) tend to love it."
Lambic
FlavourFunky, complex, very sour. Fermented with wild airborne yeast (Brettanomyces) and bacteria in the Senne Valley of Belgium. Barnyard, leather, green apple, tart. The original wild ale.
ABV5–7%
VariantsKriek — lambic with cherries (tart cherry, dry). Framboise — with raspberries. Peche — with peach.
Examples
Lindemans KriekCantillon GueuzeTimmermans Lambic
Pairs with
Aged goat cheeseDuckFruit desserts
💬 How to sell it: "Lambic is the natural wine of the beer world — fermented with wild yeast, funky, complex, and totally unique. Natural wine drinkers and adventurous foodies love it. Lindemans Kriek (cherry) is the most approachable entry point."
Gueuze
FlavourBlend of young and old lambics. Sparkling, very dry, complex sour. Lemon, green apple, oak, funk, barnyard. The Champagne of Belgium.
ABV5–8%
Examples
Cantillon Gueuze3 Fonteinen GueuzeBoon Gueuze
Pairs with
OystersMusselsAged cheeseCharcuterie
💬 How to sell it: "Gueuze is called the Champagne of Belgium — sparkling, bone dry, complex. If someone drinks natural wine or aged Champagne, this will blow their mind. Not a casual beer — it's an experience."
Fruit Sour
FlavourTart, fruity, refreshing. Real fruit added to a sour base (Berliner, Gose, or kettle sour). Tastes like sparkling fruit juice. The most approachable sour style.
ABV4–6%
Examples
Collective Arts RansackAnderson Valley Briney Melon
BC Craft
Four Winds Nectarous (Peach)Parallel 49 Sour CherryStrathcona Passionfruit SourDogwood Mixed Fruit Sour
Pairs with
DessertsLight cheesesBrunchOn its own
💬 How to sell it: "Fruit sours are the most approachable beer for non-beer drinkers — they taste like sparkling fruit juice, barely any beer flavour, refreshing and fun. If someone says they don't really drink beer, start here."
Flanders Red Ale
FlavourComplex, sour, vinous. Dark cherry, plum, chocolate, oak, balsamic. Aged in large oak barrels (foeders). Often blended. Called the "Burgundy of Belgium."
ABV5–7%
Examples
Rodenbach Grand CruDuchesse de BourgogneVerhaeghe Vichtenaar
Pairs with
DuckGame meatChocolateAged gouda
💬 How to sell it: "Flanders Red is called the Burgundy of Belgium — it's complex, sour, and wine-like. Red wine drinkers who want to try something completely different will love it. Rodenbach Grand Cru is the benchmark."
Quick tip"Never had sour beer" → Fruit Sour or Gose. "Likes natural wine" → Lambic or Gueuze. "Red wine drinker" → Flanders Red.
Belgian & SpecialtyWitbier · Saison · Trappist
Who it's forAdventurous drinkers, wine lovers, foodies. Belgian beers are the most complex and food-pairable of all styles — often compared to wine in their depth.

ABV4.5–5.5%

Tripel
FlavourGolden, strong, spicy, fruity. Banana, clove, pepper, honey, dry finish. High alcohol but deceptively drinkable. Brewed by Trappist monks.
ABV7.5–10%

Dubbel
FlavourDark, malty, fruity. Dark fruit (raisin, plum, fig), chocolate, caramel, spicy yeast. The dark counterpart to Tripel. Rich but not heavy.

FlavourPale golden, highly carbonated, deceptively strong. Fruity, spicy, dry, effervescent. Pear, apple, coriander, pepper. The Champagne of Belgian ales.

Quick tip"Wine drinker wanting beer" → Saison or Witbier. "Want something special" → Tripel or Quad. "Love dark fruit" → Dubbel. "Champagne lover" → Belgian Strong Golden.
